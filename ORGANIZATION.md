# Organization & Governance

This document outlines how the Himachali Languages Initiative is structured and how decisions are made.

## Organization Structure
- The initiative is maintained by two organization owners.
- Decisions are made collaboratively, with transparency and community interest as priorities.
- Contributors are encouraged to participate openly regardless of background.

## Contribution Principles
- Open participation and inclusivity
- Respect for linguistic diversity and dialect variations
- Community-first and non-commercial development
- Proper credit for all contributors

## Roles
- **Language Contributors:** Native or fluent speakers contributing sentences and translations
- **Technical Contributors:** Developers and researchers working on data, models, websites, and tools
- **Reviewers:** Contributors helping ensure language quality and consistency
- **Coordinators:** Members assisting with community management and outreach

## Data Ethics
- Contributions are voluntary
- No private, personal, or sensitive data is collected
- Dialect variation is natural; no single version is treated as “correct”
- All data is released under open licenses for public use

## Long-Term Vision
- Support academic research and student projects
- Enable language technology such as translation tools and learning apps
- Expand to more dialects over time
- Remain open, transparent, and community-owned
